Cipher Challenges

Cyber Squad 2026 - Day 3 Lab: Crack the Code. Work in teams. Tools allowed: pencil, the cipher wheel, and Colab.

Reminder: a Caesar cipher shifts each letter by a fixed amount. A Vigenere cipher uses a repeating keyword. Atbash flips the alphabet (A becomes Z). Base64 is encoding, not encryption - anyone can reverse it.

Challenge 1 - Caesar (shift is 3)

Decrypt this message. The shift is 3.
WKH HDJOH KDV ODQGHG

Challenge 2 - Caesar (shift unknown - crack it)

No key given. Use frequency analysis: E, T, A, O are the most common English letters.
KWJVZJSHD FSFQDXNX GWJFPX XNRUQJ HNUMJWX

Challenge 3 - Vigenere (keyword: KEY)

Each letter is shifted by the matching letter of the repeating keyword KEY.
KXRKGI KX BKAL

Challenge 4 - Atbash (mirror the alphabet)

A becomes Z, B becomes Y, and so on. The cipher is its own reverse.
SVOOL DLIOW

Challenge 5 - Base64 (decode, do not "decrypt")

Spot it by the trailing =. Use base64.b64decode(...) in Colab.
RkxBR3tjcnlwdG9faXNfZnVufQ==

Build Your Own

Encode a short message to a teammate using the cipher wheel with a shift of your choice. Swap, and try to crack each other's without being told the shift.

Answer Key (for instructors)

Challenge 1: THE EAGLE HAS LANDED (shift back 3).

Challenge 2: FREQUENCY ANALYSIS BREAKS SIMPLE CIPHERS (the shift was 5; the most common ciphertext letter J maps back to E).

Challenge 3: ATTACK AT DAWN (keyword KEY).

Challenge 4: HELLO WORLD (Atbash).

Challenge 5: FLAG{crypto_is_fun} (Base64 decode).

Teaching point: Challenges 1, 3, and 4 use a key; you still cracked 2 without one. That is why short keys and simple ciphers are unsafe, and why modern keys are huge.
