# Instructor Setup Guide (U.S. Cyber Range)

## Before the camp
1. Create an educator account at https://www.uscyberrange.org/ and confirm your institution or program is eligible.
2. Review pricing and plan options on https://www.uscyberrange.org/pricing/ and arrange access for the number of students you expect.
3. Explore the Courseware Repository and open **Course 6302: Cybersecurity Fundamentals** for ready-made lessons and exercises.
4. In the Exercise Area, identify the environments you will use each day:
   - Day 1: a basic Linux environment.
   - Day 2: a Linux environment with standard command-line tools.
   - Day 3: an environment with Wireshark and at least two communicating in-range hosts.
   - Day 4: an isolated, intentionally vulnerable training web application.
   - Day 5: a Capture The Flag or capstone exercise, plus a log set for the blue-team round.

## One week before
- Enroll students or generate invite links.
- Do a full dry run: launch each day's environment yourself and complete the student steps.
- Test that students can log in from the devices they will use.

## Each morning
- Open the day's `DayN.md` and the orientation deck.
- Pre-launch environments if your plan allows, to save class time.
- Keep the safety-and-ethics agreement visible.

## Troubleshooting
- If a student cannot reach an environment, confirm browser compatibility and that the environment finished provisioning.
- For range-specific issues, use the support resources on uscyberrange.org.
