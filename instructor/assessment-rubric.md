# Assessment Rubric

Use a simple 0 to 3 scale per item: 0 not yet, 1 emerging, 2 proficient, 3 strong.

## Daily practical checks
- Day 1: launched the Linux environment and used pwd, ls, cd, cat correctly.
- Day 2: produced two different hashes for the same word using two salts.
- Day 3: captured packets and identified one HTTP (readable) vs HTTPS (encrypted) exchange.
- Day 4: listed three OWASP Top 10 items with a defense for each.
- Day 5: contributed to the CTF score and answered the blue-team log questions.

## Knowledge checks
Each day guide ends with numericals and MCQs and an answer key. Record a score out of the number of questions.

## Capstone (Day 5)
- Team CTF flags found (count).
- Blue-team triage: identified the suspicious account, the timing, and a sensible response.
- Showcase: clearly explained one challenge and one defense.

## Participation and ethics
- Followed the safety and ethics agreement at all times.
- Helped teammates and asked good questions.

## Suggested weighting
Daily practicals 40 percent, knowledge checks 20 percent, capstone 30 percent, participation and ethics 10 percent.
